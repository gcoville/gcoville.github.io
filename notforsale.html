Frequently asked questions about gc.org:

Q: Is gc.org for sale?

A: No.

Q: But you don't seem to be doing much with it.

A: There's more to the internet than the World Wide Web. In addition to the small web page here, I have been using the domain for email for many years.

Q: But my organization/initials/company/favoriteletters are "gc". I am entitled to gc.org. Why won't you sell it?

A: No, you're not entitled to it. I got to it first. I won't sell it because I've been using it for email for many years.

Q: Okay, so gc.org isn't for sale. But everything has a price.

A: True enough. You would have to offer enough to overcome the hassle I would have to go through in migrating all my email presence away from gc.org. Keep in mind, I've had this domain for 3 decades and I'm quite attached to it. In the past (even during the domain boom of 2000), many folks came to the conclusion that the cost of buying this domain wasn't worth it. Look, even though it's a 2-letter second level domain, it's still only in the ".org" TLD. (Gosh, I just wish I'd gotten gc.com so many years ago.)

Q: What's so special about gc.org to you, anyway?

A: My initials are GC.

Q: Can I just have an email address in gc.org, and it's gotta have good spam filtering, and maybe some web hosting, and it's gotta have the latest version of PHP installed, and can you help me with some ruby programming?

A: No, I don't want to be responsible for your email address or your web hosting. My ruby skills are minimal at best.

Q: How about a subdomain of gc.org?

A: No.

Q: How do I contact you by email?

A: I have a Contact page where you can determine my email address.
